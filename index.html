How Bitrate Calculation Works

Everything you need to understand OBS bitrate settings and streaming quality.

What is bitrate in live streaming?

Bitrate is the amount of video and audio data sent to your streaming platform every second, measured in megabits per second (Mbps) or kilobits per second (Kbps). A higher bitrate generally means sharper, cleaner video — but it also demands more upload bandwidth. Setting the wrong bitrate is the most common reason for a stream that buffers, drops frames, or looks blurry.

The bitrate calculator formula

GamingUP27 Bitrate Calculator uses a simple, proven formula used by streamers on Twitch, YouTube Live, Facebook Gaming and Kick:

  • Recommended Bitrate = Upload Speed ÷ Safety Buffer
  • Safety buffer of 1.5x–2x accounts for network fluctuation, packet loss and background bandwidth usage.
  • Audio bitrate (128–320 kbps) is calculated separately and added on top of video bitrate.

Why upload speed and not download speed?

Streaming sends data from your device to the platform, so your upload speed is what matters — not download speed. Always test your real upload speed with a speed test tool before setting your OBS bitrate, since real-world speeds are often lower than what your ISP advertises.

OBS bitrate settings tips

  • Use CBR (Constant Bitrate) rate control in OBS for the most stable stream on Twitch and YouTube.
  • Keyframe interval should be set to 2 seconds for best platform compatibility.
  • If your stream drops frames, lower your bitrate by 10–15% before changing resolution.
  • Wired ethernet connections are more stable for streaming than Wi-Fi.
What bitrate should I use for 1080p60 streaming?+

For 1080p at 60fps, a bitrate between 6–9 Mbps is recommended by most platforms including Twitch and YouTube Live, provided your upload speed is at least 9–13 Mbps.

Why does Twitch limit bitrate to 8.5 Mbps?+

Twitch caps bitrate to keep streams watchable for viewers on slower connections worldwide and to manage server load, regardless of how fast your internet connection is.

Is a higher bitrate always better for streaming?+

No. Going above a platform's cap or your stable upload speed causes dropped frames and buffering for viewers. The goal is the highest bitrate your connection can sustain reliably, not the highest number possible.

How do I test my real upload speed?+

Use a speed test tool such as Speedtest.net or Fast.com. Run it a few times at different times of day, and use the lowest result to calculate a safe streaming bitrate.

What is a good clicks per second (CPS) score?+

Most people average around 5–7 clicks per second. Scores of 8–10 CPS are considered fast, and competitive clickers using techniques like jitter or butterfly clicking can reach 12+ CPS.

Is the DPI checker 100% accurate?+

No tool running in a browser can read your mouse's raw hardware DPI directly. This checker gives a close estimate based on on-screen pixel movement versus real physical distance. For an exact reading, check your mouse manufacturer's official software.
